Can the Number of NYC Residents who have Received their Flu Shot Indicate How Many People Will Get the COVID-19 Vaccination?

By Renee Onque | December 13, 2020

Source: CDC/Alissa Eckert

While it usually takes 2-5 years of discovery alone to develop a vaccination, the CDC approved the COVID-19 vaccine on Dec. 13, after less than a year of research.

Often, the coronavirus is compared to the 1918-19 Spanish flu; therefore, recent flu shot data for NYC may be indicative of how many residents will receive the COVID-19 vaccination.

The percentages were very low across the five boroughs, but a recent poll showed that more people are willing to get their COVID-19 shot.

"We should keep in mind that this pandemic is causing disease and death on a sclae that has not been seen since the great influenza pandemic of 1918," said Dr. Paul Spearman in an interview with SciLine.

It is evident that the flu wasn't as alarming for citizens in 2017, as the coronavirus is in 2020, and the data cannot determine how many people will get vaccinated.
